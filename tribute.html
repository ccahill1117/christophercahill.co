Here's a timeline of Robert Burns Woodward's life:

  • 1917 - Born in Boston, MA.
  • 1933 - Enters Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).
  • 1934 - Excluded from MIT due to inattention to formal studies.
  • 1935 - Readmitted to MIT in the fall term.
  • 1936 - Receives Bachelor of science degree.
  • 1937 - In only one year, receives a doctorate while most of his colleagues from bachelor studies were still completing their undergraduate degrees. Woodward's doctoral work was related mostly to synthesis of the female sex hormone, estrone.
  • 1944 - Reported synthesis of alkaloid quinone, used to treat malaria, with post-doctoral researcher, William von Eggers Doering.

The most brilliant analysis ever done on a structural puzzle was surely the solution (1953) of the terramycin problem. It was a problem of great industrial importance, and hence many able chemists had performed an enormous amount of work trying to determine the structure. There seemed to be too much data to resolve the problem, because a significant number of observations, although experimentally correct, were very misleading. Woodward took a large piece of cardboard, wrote on it all the facts and, by thought alone, deduced the correct structure for terramycin. Nobody else could have done that at the time.

Sir Derek Harold Richard Barton, Nobel Prize Laureate
